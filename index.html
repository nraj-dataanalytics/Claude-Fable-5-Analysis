// How We Got Here

From a Restricted Preview to a Public Frontier Model

Claude Fable 5 didn't appear out of nowhere. It's the public face of a model Anthropic spent months treating very differently — here's the path it took.

STAGE 1 — APRIL 2026

Mythos Preview: A Restricted Research Tier

Anthropic introduced Claude Mythos Preview as a research-preview model with capabilities well beyond its public lineup — particularly in cybersecurity analysis. Rather than a general release, Anthropic launched Project Glasswing: a coalition of vetted organizations including AWS, Apple, Google, Microsoft, and CrowdStrike, using the model to find and help fix vulnerabilities across critical software. In six weeks, partners identified more than 10,000 high-severity issues.

APRIL 7, 2026 · RESEARCH PREVIEW
STAGE 2 — JUNE 9, 2026

Fable 5: Mythos-Class, Made Public

Anthropic released Claude Fable 5 — built on the same underlying model as Mythos, with safety classifiers tuned for general use. It became the first model to break 90% on Hex's complex analytics benchmark, ranked #1 on Hebbia's finance reasoning benchmark, and posted 80.3% on SWE-Bench Pro. It launched across claude.ai, the API, Snowflake, Databricks, AWS, and GitHub Copilot.

JUNE 9, 2026 · PUBLIC LAUNCH
STAGE 3 — JUNE 12–13, 2026

A Pause for Regulatory Review

Days after launch, Anthropic received a US government export control directive related to Fable 5 and Mythos 5 access. Because the order applied to a category of users Anthropic could not isolate technically, the company paused access to both models for everyone while it works through the requirement. Anthropic's other models were not affected, and the company says it is working toward restoring access.

JUNE 12–13, 2026 · ACCESS PAUSED

// Where Things Stand

Current Access Status

Quick reference for anyone planning work around Fable 5 — what's available today and what to expect.

As of June 13, 2026

Access to Claude Fable 5 and Claude Mythos 5 is temporarily paused while Anthropic addresses a US government export control directive received on June 12, 2026 — three days after Fable 5's public launch. The directive relates to access controls for certain user categories, and because Anthropic could not apply that distinction in real time across its products, it paused both models globally rather than partially.

Anthropic has stated it disagrees with the underlying concern (described as a narrow, low-severity issue) but is complying while it works through the requirement, and is aiming to restore access as soon as possible. No restoration date has been set.

What's unaffected: Claude Opus 4.8, Sonnet 4.6, and Haiku 4.5 remain fully available across claude.ai, the API, and all integrations — including Snowflake, Databricks, and GitHub Copilot.

Fable 5 Public Access Window

Originally planned vs. actual — days of access from launch
Anthropic had planned to include Fable 5 free on Pro/Max/Team plans through June 22, 2026 (13 days). Following the directive received on June 12, 2026, that window paused after 3 days. Anthropic says it intends to resume the rollout once the requirement is resolved.
// For Data Analysts

What This Means for Data Analytics Work

Understanding what Fable 5 demonstrated — and preparing to use it — is exactly the kind of forward thinking that sets strong analysts apart.

RIGHT NOW

What You Can Use Today

Claude Opus 4.8, Sonnet 4.6, and Haiku 4.5 are all available without interruption. Opus 4.8 remains a top-tier model for analytical work — scoring 69.2% on SWE-Bench Pro and around 80% on the Hex analytics benchmark. It's a strong foundation for document QA, financial analysis, and reporting while Fable 5 access resumes.

ANALYTICS

Long-Running Analysis

Fable 5 was the first model to break 90% on Hex's complex analytical tasks benchmark — a 10-point jump over the prior best. That gap is the difference between a model that loses coherence halfway through a 200-page analysis and one that finishes it accurately and self-verifies along the way.

FINANCE

Financial Document Reasoning

Fable 5 ranked #1 on Hebbia's Finance Benchmark — tested on earnings reports, 10-Ks, charts, tables, and financial models. IMC reported it performed strongly across trading-analysis evaluations including factual lookup, root-cause analysis, and expected-value analysis. This is the use case most worth preparing for.

DOCUMENT AI

PDF, Chart & Table Understanding

A large share of business data lives in PDFs with embedded charts. Fable 5 scored 29.8% on visual document reasoning — ahead of GPT-5.5 (24.9%) and Opus 4.8 (22.5%). Databricks measured +20% accuracy on complex doc QA vs. Opus 4.8, using 12% fewer tool calls.

SQL

Snowflake Native Integration

Fable 5 was made callable directly in Snowflake Cortex AI via AI_COMPLETE('claude-fable-5', ...), letting analysts run AI analysis directly in SQL. This integration is expected to resume alongside general access — worth building toward now.

STRATEGY

How to Prepare

Watch status.anthropic.com for updates. Build prompts, workflows, and use cases on Opus 4.8 now so they're ready to point at Fable 5 the moment access resumes — the analysts who plan ahead will be the ones who move fastest.

// Complete Timeline

Every Key Event — In Order

The full sequence behind one of 2026's most significant AI capability stories.

MARCH 26, 2026

Mythos Surfaces Ahead of Announcement

Leaked blog post drafts reveal the existence of a model named Claude Mythos before any official announcement — an early signal Anthropic was preparing something it would treat differently from its standard lineup.

APRIL 7–8, 2026

Project Glasswing Launches — Mythos Preview Reaches 12 Founding Partners

AWS, Apple, Google, Microsoft, Cisco, CrowdStrike, NVIDIA, JPMorganChase, Palo Alto Networks, Broadcom, Linux Foundation, and Anthropic form the coalition. 40+ additional vetted organizations also receive access. The model is used for defensive cybersecurity research.

APRIL — MAY 2026

10,000+ Vulnerabilities Identified in Six Weeks

Glasswing partners report more than 10,000 high or critical-severity vulnerabilities found. 23,019 candidates scanned across 1,000+ open-source projects, with 1,726 confirmed (90.8% true positive rate) — including long-standing issues in OpenBSD, FreeBSD, and FFmpeg.

MAY — JUNE 2026

Glasswing Expands to 150+ Organizations Across 15+ Countries

The program scales globally. Claude Security launches in public beta for Enterprise customers — powered by Opus 4.7, it patches 2,100+ vulnerabilities in its first three weeks.

JUNE 4, 2026

Anthropic Publishes a Call for Coordinated AI Safety Measures

Anthropic releases "When AI Builds Itself," a paper calling for globally coordinated safeguards on frontier AI development — published five days before its most capable public model launch.

JUNE 9, 2026 — LAUNCH

Claude Fable 5 Launches Publicly

The first Mythos-class model available to anyone. 90%+ on Hex analytics. #1 on Hebbia Finance. 80.3% on SWE-Bench Pro. Available on claude.ai, the API, AWS Bedrock, Google Cloud, Snowflake, Databricks, and GitHub Copilot — free on all paid plans through June 22.

JUNE 12, 2026

US Export Control Directive Received

At 5:21pm ET, Anthropic receives a directive from the Commerce Department's Bureau of Industry and Security relating to access controls for Fable 5 and Mythos 5. Anthropic states it disagrees with the underlying concern, which it considers minor, but begins working through the requirement immediately.

JUNE 12–13, 2026 — TODAY

Access to Fable 5 and Mythos 5 Paused Globally

Both models are paused for all users while the requirement is addressed. All other Claude models remain fully operational. Anthropic says it is working toward restoring access and will share updates via its status page.

Why I Tracked This — And What I Found

I have been working in business analytics and data operations for long enough to know what a real capability shift looks like — versus what is just a benchmark press release. When Mythos Preview was announced in April, I read every technical detail. Not because of the cybersecurity angle. Because a model that demonstrated capabilities beyond expert human performance in a specific domain — without being trained specifically for it — told me something important about where reasoning AI was heading.

When Fable 5 launched on June 9th, I understood immediately what the analytics numbers meant in practice. A 10-point jump on the Hex benchmark is not incremental. It is the difference between a model that stays coherent through a 200-page financial report and one that drifts off-track by page 50. The Hebbia Finance #1 ranking, the Databricks +20% document QA accuracy, and the Snowflake SQL integration weren't abstract improvements — they were the exact bottlenecks I run into every week in real analytical work.

Then, three days after launch, access paused for regulatory reasons. That timing is part of the story too — it's a reminder that access to frontier AI capability can shift quickly, and that the analysts who understand what a model can do, and plan around it, stay ahead regardless of when access changes.

This entire project — research, website, analysis, data files — was built in one session using Claude. That's the point. The workflow Fable 5 was designed to unlock already exists. I'm building toward it.
