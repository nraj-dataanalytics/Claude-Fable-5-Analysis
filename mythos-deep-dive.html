// What is it

A Model Class That Changed the Calculus

Claude Mythos Preview is Anthropic's most advanced AI model. It was not built specifically for cybersecurity — its security capabilities emerged as a byproduct of general improvements in code, reasoning, and autonomous task execution.

🧠

General Intelligence, Security Superpower

Mythos was trained as a general-purpose model. Its ability to find and exploit vulnerabilities was never the target — it emerged from better reasoning and coding ability. That's what surprised even Anthropic.

🔒

Why It's Not Available to You

If released publicly, it could enable almost anyone — at very low cost — to find and exploit unknown security flaws in operating systems, browsers, and critical software. That risk was deemed too high.

🌐

What Project Glasswing Is

Named after the glasswing butterfly (transparent wings, hiding in plain sight), it's a controlled cybersecurity coalition where vetted organizations use Mythos Preview exclusively for defensive purposes.

⚡

The Speed Shift

Security work that once took expert human teams weeks or months can now be done by Mythos in hours, for tens to hundreds of dollars in compute. That speed asymmetry changes everything.

Closed-Source Reverse Engineering

Mythos can reverse-engineer stripped binaries (compiled software with no source code) and find exploitable flaws. It did this on browsers and operating systems where no source access exists.

// The Decision

Why Anthropic Chose Not to Release It

Three interconnected reasons form the logic behind the most significant product withholding in AI history.

01

The Dual-Use Problem

The same capability that lets defenders find and fix vulnerabilities lets attackers find and exploit them. Unlike most dual-use AI risks, here the attack surface is every device, OS, and browser on the planet — and the attack cost drops to nearly zero.

02

The 6–12 Month Window

Anthropic publicly stated: within 6 to 12 months, other AI companies will have Mythos-class models and may release them without safeguards. The goal of Glasswing is to give defenders a head start before that window closes.

03

99% of Bugs Are Still Unpatched

Of the vulnerabilities found, the vast majority have not yet been fixed. Publishing technical details before patches ship would hand attackers a ready-made exploit list. Responsible disclosure requires silence — for now.

// Scenario Analysis

What Could Have Happened — and What This Means

The decision to restrict Mythos is historically significant. Here's the branching path of outcomes.

// Scenario A — If It Had Been Released Publicly

The Risk That Was Avoided

Any individual, criminal group, or nation-state would have access to a tool that can autonomously find exploitable vulnerabilities in Windows, macOS, iOS, Android, Chrome, and Firefox for ~$100. Ransomware attacks, government espionage, and infrastructure takedowns would scale dramatically. The patch-vs-exploit race, already unwinnable for defenders, would have collapsed.

// Scenario B — If Glasswing Had Not Existed

The Opportunity That Would Have Been Missed

Anthropic would have had to either sit on the model indefinitely or release it anyway. The 10,000+ vulnerabilities now being patched across critical infrastructure would have remained open. The project forced an industry-wide acknowledgment that AI has crossed an offensive threshold — and created the coordination structure to respond.

// Scenario C — What Happens in 6–12 Months

The Race Anthropic Is Trying to Win

Anthropic expects competitors to build Mythos-class models and release them openly. When that happens, the security gaps Glasswing is patching today will no longer exist — but any that remain open will be immediately exploitable by anyone. The value of the head start is measured in vulnerabilities patched before that moment arrives.

// Scenario D — The Precedent It Sets

AI Industry Behavior May Never Be the Same

For the first time, a major AI lab built its most powerful model and chose not to sell it. Whether this is genuine safety leadership or brilliant marketing (exclusivity makes Mythos the most talked-about model in AI history), it demonstrates that the "ship everything" norm can be broken — and invites regulation and norms to form around the idea that some AI capabilities require restricted access.
